About the Public Distribution System

The Public Distribution System (PDS) is an Indian food security system established by the Government of India. It distributes subsidized food and non-food items to India's poor. Major commodities distributed include staple food grains, sugar, and kerosene through a network of fair price shops.

The system aims to ensure food security and equitable distribution of resources. Beneficiaries receive their entitlements through ration cards. The system also helps in stabilizing prices and alleviating poverty in the country.
